Origin Story

Intro: Riomaggiore's story begins with refugees seeking safety in a remote coastal valley.
Headline: From Greek Refugees to Ligurian Gem
Full Story

In the 8th century, Greek refugees fleeing barbarian invasions in the Vara Valley discovered this sheltered inlet. They established a small fishing community, gradually building the terraced hillsides we see today. The village's name derives from 'Rivus Major' (major river), referring to the stream that flows through the valley to the sea.

Name Etymology
Origin: Latin 'Rivus Major'
Meaning: Major river or main stream
Original Name: Rivus Major
Founding Legend: Legend says the first settlers were guided to this hidden cove by dolphins who showed them safe passage through the rocky coastline.

Section Intro

Riomaggiore's history spans over eight centuries, evolving from a medieval fishing settlement to the gateway of the world-famous Cinque Terre. Its strategic position and natural harbor shaped its development as both a maritime community and agricultural center.

Historical Periods

Birth of a Fishing Village
Intro: The formative centuries when Riomaggiore established its maritime identity.
Years: 1239-1400
Headline: Birth of a Fishing Village
Description

During this period, Riomaggiore developed as a typical Ligurian fishing village. Residents built the first terraced vineyards and olive groves on the steep hillsides, while the harbor became central to daily life. The village was governed by local maritime laws that regulated fishing rights and trade.
