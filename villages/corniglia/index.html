Origin Story

Intro: Corniglia's story begins with Roman recognition of its perfect wine-growing conditions.
Headline: The High Village of Ancient Vines
Full Story

Roman settlers chose this elevated site for its exceptional viticulture potential - southern exposure, well-draining soil, and protection from sea storms. Unlike the other villages that developed around harbors, Corniglia grew as a purely agricultural community. The village's name likely derives from 'Cornelia,' possibly referring to the Roman gens (family) Cornelia who may have owned estates here, or from 'cornu' (horn) referring to the horn-shaped promontory.

Name Etymology
Origin: Latin - either personal or geographical reference
Meaning: Either from Roman family name 'Cornelia' or 'horn-shaped promontory'
Original Name: Cornelia or Cornu
Founding Legend: Legend says Roman wine merchants chose this spot because eagles circling overhead showed them the perfect location for vineyards that would produce wines fit for the gods.

From Fishing Village To Tourism

Headline: Agricultural Village Discovers Tourism
Challenges

  • Young people leaving for urban areas

Modern Balance

Today, Corniglia maintains the strongest agricultural character of all five villages. Many residents continue traditional farming while others work in tourism, creating a unique balance of authentic rural life and visitor services.

Transition Period

Tourism developed more slowly in Corniglia due to its elevated position and agricultural focus. The village remained primarily agricultural until the UNESCO designation brought international attention.

Traditional Economy

Unlike other Cinque Terre villages, Corniglia was never primarily a fishing community. Its economy centered entirely on agriculture - wine production, olive cultivation, and small-scale farming on the extensive terraced hillsides.
