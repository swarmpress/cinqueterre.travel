Origin Story

Intro: Manarola's story begins with Roman recognition of its perfect wine-growing conditions.
Headline: From Roman Vines to Global Icon
Full Story

The Romans first established terraced vineyards on these steep cliffs, recognizing the ideal combination of maritime climate, well-draining soil, and southern exposure. Over centuries, the village grew around this wine-making tradition, with families passing down both vineyard plots and wine-making knowledge through generations. The village's name likely derives from 'Manium arula' (small altar to the Manes spirits) or from 'magna rota' (great wheel), referring to the mill wheel that once powered the village.

Name Etymology
Origin: Latin - uncertain between religious or industrial origin
Meaning: Either 'small altar' or 'great wheel'
Founding Legend: Local legend claims the village was founded by Roman soldiers who fell in love with the landscape and decided to stay and plant vines rather than continue their campaigns.

Section Intro

Manarola's history as a wine-producing village dates back to Roman times, making it possibly the oldest of the Cinque Terre settlements. Its compact size and dramatic setting have shaped a unique community centered around viticulture and the sea.

Birth of Terraced Viticulture
Intro: Romans establish the foundation of Manarola's wine-making tradition.
Years: 1st century BCE - 5th century CE
Headline: Birth of Terraced Viticulture
Description

Roman settlers constructed the first terraced vineyards, recognizing Manarola's exceptional wine-growing potential. They developed the basic infrastructure of dry-stone walls and terraces that still support modern wine production. The Roman villa rustica model influenced the village's agricultural organization.
