Human Perspective

Cinque Terre deserves to be shown by people who listen more than they promote. Every recommendation on this site is filtered through human perspective.

We are not experts claiming authority, but attentive guides offering context. We believe that what you read should be shaped by lived experience, attention, and care.

Lead Editor & Local Expert

Giulia Rossi

Born and raised in Riomaggiore, Giulia knows every stone of the village. After years abroad studying journalism, she returned to tell the stories of her home with fresh eyes and deep roots.

How they move

Early mornings on the harbor rocks. Coffee at Bar Centrale. Walks without destination.

What they love

The sound of the sea at night. The way the village smells after rain. Her grandmother's focaccia recipe.

Grounded Practices

Trail running Local wine curation Preserving oral histories
Food & Culinary Editor

Marco Bianchi

A former chef who traded the kitchen for the page. Marco believes that food is memory, and that every dish in the Cinque Terre tells a story of the land and the sea.

How they move

Long conversations with fishermen. Searching for the perfect pesto. Cooking for friends.

What they love

The first taste of new wine in October. Fish still glistening from the net. The patience of bread rising.

Grounded Practices

Foraging Wine pairing Teaching cooking classes
Visual Editor & Photographer

Elena Moretti

Elena captures the Cinque Terre in light and shadow. Her photographs seek to show the villages as the locals see them—not postcard perfection, but the quiet beauty of everyday life.

How they move

Golden hour on the cliffs. Watching, waiting, noticing. The world through a lens.

What they love

The way afternoon light turns the houses amber. Empty streets at dawn. The patience of a good shot.

Grounded Practices

Film photography Watercolor sketching Hiking with her dog
A Shared Attention

The Art of Collaboration

This site is shaped by conversation, disagreement, and shared attention. It is not a static resource, but a living record of our evolving relationship with Cinque Terre.

We invite you to read these pages knowing who is speaking. Move through the villages with a sense of familiarity rather than authority, and allow the place to reveal itself to you in its own time.
