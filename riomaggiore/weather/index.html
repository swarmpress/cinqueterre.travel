Month-by-Month Weather Guide

Understanding Riomaggiore's weather patterns ensures you pack appropriately and plan the perfect activities for your visit.

Best Overall Weather: May & September These shoulder months offer the perfect balance of warm temperatures (20-24°C), minimal rainfall, and fewer crowds. Restaurants are fully operational, hiking trails are comfortable, and the Mediterranean sparkles under clear skies.

Peak Summer (June-August) Expect temperatures between 25-28°C with high humidity. While perfect for swimming and evening dining on seaside terraces, midday hiking can be challenging. Book restaurants like Dau Cila well in advance for sunset dining.

Shoulder Seasons (March-April, October-November) Spring brings wildflowers and comfortable 15-20°C temperatures, ideal for exploring coastal paths. Autumn offers harvest season charm with pleasant 18-22°C weather, though some restaurants may have reduced hours.

Winter Considerations (December-February) Mild but unpredictable weather with temperatures around 8-13°C. Many establishments close, including some top restaurants. However, winter offers dramatic coastal storms and peaceful village exploration.

What's the best month to visit Riomaggiore for hiking?

May and September offer ideal hiking conditions with comfortable temperatures (20-24°C), minimal rainfall, and clear skies. Trails are less crowded than summer months, and you'll enjoy perfect conditions for the coastal paths and vineyard terraces.

How hot does it get in summer?

Summer temperatures typically range from 25-28°C (77-82°F) with high humidity. Sea breezes provide natural air conditioning, especially along the waterfront. Early morning and evening are most comfortable for activities like hiking or exploring the village streets.

Does it rain much in Riomaggiore?

Riomaggiore receives most rainfall between November and March, with occasional summer thunderstorms. The village averages 850mm annually, concentrated in winter months. Summer (June-August) is typically dry with only 3-4 rainy days per month.

What should I pack for different seasons?

Spring/Fall: Layers, light rain jacket, comfortable walking shoes. Summer: Lightweight clothing, sun hat, reef-safe sunscreen, sandals. Winter: Warm layers, waterproof jacket, sturdy shoes for potentially wet streets.

Are restaurants open year-round?

Many restaurants close December-February, including Rio Bistrot and Dau Cila. Te la do io la merenda typically remains open year-round. Always check current hours as seasonal closures can vary based on weather and local conditions.

Can weather affect transportation to Riomaggiore?

High winds or storms can occasionally disrupt ferry services between villages. The train service is generally reliable year-round. Via dell'Amore walking path may close during rough weather for safety reasons.
