Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get to Riomaggiore from La Spezia?

Take the Cinque Terre Express train from La Spezia Centrale—it's just 3 minutes to Riomaggiore and trains run every 15-20 minutes from 5:00 AM to midnight. Purchase your Cinque Terre Card at the station for €16 (one day) which includes unlimited train rides and trail access. The station is a 2-minute walk uphill to the village center.

Is the Via dell'Amore currently open?

The Via dell'Amore has been closed since 2012 due to rockfall safety concerns. The full coastal path is expected to reopen in late 2024, with a new safety tunnel and upgraded walkway. Currently, you can walk the first 200 meters from Riomaggiore for photos, but the complete romantic walk to Manarola is not accessible.

Where should I eat in Riomaggiore for the best experience?

For fine dining, book Rio Bistrot (Michelin Guide listed) with harbor views—call +39 0187 920616 well in advance. For romance, try Dau Cila's seaside terrace (+39 0187 760032). For authentic local experience, grab focaccia col formaggio from Te la do io la merenda near the station—it's the village's best-kept secret and costs under €5.

What's the best time to visit Riomaggiore?

Late September through October offers warm weather, fewer crowds, and open restaurants. Summer (July-August) is busiest with all amenities open but expect crowds. November-March sees restaurant closures and limited train service. For photography, arrive early morning (7-9 AM) or golden hour (5-7 PM) when the colorful houses glow beautifully.

Can I drive to Riomaggiore and where do I park?

Yes, but parking is extremely limited and expensive (€3-5 per hour). Use the main parking area above the village, then walk 5 minutes downhill. Better option: park in La Spezia (€1 per hour) and take the 3-minute train. The village center is completely pedestrian-only, making walking the primary way to explore.

Which hiking trails start from Riomaggiore?

The Riomaggiore to Portovenere coastal trail (3.5 hours, moderate difficulty) offers spectacular views but requires proper hiking shoes. The easier Riomaggiore to Sanctuary trail (45 minutes uphill) provides village panoramas. Via dell'Amore to Manarola remains closed. Always check current trail conditions at the visitor center before hiking.

Essential Planning Tips

Insider knowledge that makes the difference between tourist and traveler

🚂

Train Strategy

Buy Cinque Terre Cards at machines to skip lines. Validate tickets before boarding to avoid €50 fines.

🍽️

Dining Reservations

Call restaurants directly—online booking isn't common. Lunch is typically 12:30-15:00, dinner 19:00-22:30.

📱

Connectivity

WiFi is limited. Download offline maps and train schedules. Most restaurants accept cards, but carry cash for small shops.

🎒

What to Bring

Comfortable walking shoes are essential. The village is all stairs and steep paths. Bring layers—weather changes quickly.
