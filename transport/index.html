Transportation Options

Cinque Terre Regional Train Service

Choose Your Perfect Transportation Style

Your transportation choice shapes your entire Cinque Terre experience. The regional train offers efficiency and frequency, whisking you between villages in minutes with departures every 20-30 minutes during peak season. For a more romantic approach, seasonal ferries provide breathtaking coastal perspectives, operating from March to October with the most frequent service from May through September.

Walking the ancient sentieri (trails) connects you intimately with the landscape, though prepare for challenging terrain and varying closure schedules due to weather and maintenance. The famous Via dell'Amore remains closed indefinitely, but alternative coastal paths offer equally stunning experiences.

Pro tip: Purchase the Cinque Terre Card for unlimited train travel, trail access, and Wi-Fi at stations. The daily card (€16) pays for itself after just two train journeys, and the multi-day options provide even better value for extended stays.

Transportation Questions Answered

Should I buy train tickets in advance?

Not necessary for regional trains between villages - they don't sell out and you can buy tickets at machines or validate Cinque Terre Cards. However, book high-speed trains to/from major cities like Rome or Florence well in advance for better prices.

Can I bring luggage on the regional trains?

Yes, but space is limited. Large suitcases can be challenging during peak times when trains are crowded. Consider luggage transfer services or pack light. There are no dedicated luggage areas on these short trains.

What happens if trails are closed due to weather?

Trail closures are common, especially in winter and after heavy rain. Always check current status at park offices or online before setting out. Trains provide reliable backup transportation - this is why the Cinque Terre Card covering both is so valuable.

Are ferries worth the extra cost?

Absolutely, for the experience and perspective. The coastal views are spectacular and you'll appreciate the villages from the sea. Ferry tickets are €10-35 depending on route. They operate March-October with most frequent service May-September.

How early should I start traveling between villages?

First trains run around 6:00 AM, with service every 20-30 minutes during high season. Start early to avoid crowds, especially for photography and trail hiking. Last trains typically run until midnight in summer, but check current schedules.

Is parking available in the villages?

Very limited and expensive (€3-5/hour where available). Monterosso has the most parking options. Better to park in La Spezia (€15-20/day) and take trains, or use the parking in Levanto. The villages are largely car-free zones.
