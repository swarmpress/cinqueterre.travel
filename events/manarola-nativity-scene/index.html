Manarola Luminous Nativity Scene

Name: Manarola Luminous Nativity Scene

Details

History

Started in 1961 when Mario Andreoli lit three lights on a cross to honor a promise to his dying father. Over 60 years, he expanded it into a masterpiece using his railway worker skills and recycled materials including iron, windows, old signs, and tanks.

Organizer

Name: Associazione Presepe di Manarola Mario Andreoli
Type: Non-Profit
Website: https://www.facebook.com/presepemanarola/
Significance: World's largest lighted nativity scene, symbol of dedication, community, and sustainable art. Represents the dialogue between human creativity and the wine landscape.
Official Event: Yes

Description Long

Created by retired railway worker Mario Andreoli starting in 1961, this extraordinary nativity scene cascades down the hillside above Manarola. With over 300 life-sized figures made entirely from recycled materials and illuminated by more than 17,000 lights powered by renewable energy, it entered the Guinness World Records in 2007. The installation transforms the terraced vineyards into a stunning work of environmental art, blending faith, creativity, and sustainability. Andreoli dedicated his life to this project until his passing in 2022, and it continues through the Associazione Presepe di Manarola.

Tradition Origin: Modern
Year Established: 1961
Description Short: The world's largest illuminated nativity scene featuring over 300 figures and 17,000 lights spread across terraced hillside, created from recycled materials.
