Festa di Santa Margherita d'Antiochia

Name: Festa di Santa Margherita d'Antiochia

Details

History

Santa Margherita's bones reportedly washed ashore in 11th century. A church was built, but relics were lost in a storm. When they reappeared in 1308, the current Gothic church was constructed. The saint was a 4th century Christian martyr from Antioch.

Organizer

Name: Parish of Vernazza and Pro Loco
Type: Religious
Significance: Vernazza's most important religious and cultural celebration, honoring the patron saint whose relics define the village's spiritual identity
Official Event: Yes

Description Long

The most anticipated day of the year for Vernazza residents, this festival celebrates the village's patron saint with deep-rooted traditions. According to legend, Santa Margherita's bones washed ashore in the 11th century, leading to the construction of the beautiful Gothic church. The celebration features an evening procession starting from the main square and winding through the village streets, traditional food stalls with local specialties, live music filling the air, and concludes with spectacular fireworks launched over the harbor - a breathtaking sight visible throughout the village. The community comes together to honor centuries of tradition and faith.

Tradition Origin: Religious
Year Established: 1308
