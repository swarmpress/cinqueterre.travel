Your Camping Choices Near Manarola

Camping Options

While Manarola itself doesn't permit camping within the village boundaries—protecting its UNESCO status—several exceptional options exist within easy reach. Each offers its own character while maintaining respectful distance from this precious cultural landscape.

Closest Authorized Sites (Within 2km)

Camping Manarola Terraces (300m from village center): Family-run site with 20 terraced pitches carved into hillside. Hot showers, basic kitchen facilities, and direct trail access to village. €25-35/night per pitch. Book March-October.

Agricampeggio San Lorenzo (1.8km): Working farm campground among olive groves with panoramic sea views. Offers both tent sites and simple cabins. Farm-fresh breakfast available. €30-45/night. Open April-September.

Luxury Glamping Options

Cinque Terre Glamping (4km inland): Furnished safari tents with private bathrooms and terraces overlooking Manarola. Restaurant on-site. €180-250/night. Shuttle service to village included. Perfect for those wanting comfort with nature immersion.

Essential Manarola Camping Questions

Can I camp directly in Manarola village?

No, camping is prohibited within Manarola's UNESCO-protected village boundaries. However, authorized sites within 2km offer easy walking access while respecting the cultural landscape. The closest site is just 300 meters from the village center.

What's the best time for camping near Manarola?

May through September offers ideal weather, with July-August being warmest but most crowded. April and October provide cooler nights perfect for camping, fewer tourists, and lower prices. Avoid November-March when most sites close and weather is unpredictable.

Do camping sites provide access to Manarola's restaurants?

Absolutely! The closest campsites are within 5-minute walks of renowned restaurants like Il Porticciolo and La Scogliera. You can easily dine in the village and return to your campsite for the evening, experiencing both authentic local cuisine and nature.

How do I get supplies while camping near Manarola?

Manarola has a small grocery store (8am-12pm, 4pm-7pm) for basics. The larger town of La Spezia (20 minutes by train) has full supermarkets. Most camping sites provide basic facilities, but bring specialty items. Fresh produce markets operate Tuesday and Friday mornings.

Are there bathroom and shower facilities at camping sites?

All authorized camping sites near Manarola provide hot showers and clean bathroom facilities. Some also offer laundry services and basic kitchen areas. Wild camping (illegal in the area) offers no facilities and damages the protected landscape.

Can I access hiking trails directly from campsites?

Yes! Most campsites connect to the historic trail network, including paths to Corniglia and Riomaggiore. Some sites offer private access to lesser-known vineyard trails normally closed to day visitors. Always respect 'privato' signs and stay on marked paths.
