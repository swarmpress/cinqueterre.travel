Oratory of San Rocco

Name: Oratory of San Rocco
Rank: 16

Tips

Photo Spots: Simple stone chapel exterior, with castle backdrop, panoramic views from the upper area, architectural details of medieval stonework

Insider Tips

Visit in combination with the castle for a complete medieval historical experience. The simple architecture contrasts beautifully with the dramatic coastal setting. Best viewed as part of the castle climb.

Details

Teaser: Historic 15th-century votive chapel built during plague outbreak with panoramic castle views

History

Built in the 1480s by the local community as a votive chapel during a devastating plague epidemic that affected the Cinque Terre region. The oratory was dedicated to San Rocco, who was invoked for protection against plague and pestilence, demonstrating the community's collective response to crisis through faith.

Description

A small but historically significant 15th-century oratory built in the 1480s during a plague outbreak as a votive offering for protection. This simple stone chapel dedicated to Saint Rocco, patron saint of plague victims, represents the deep faith and community spirit of medieval Riomaggiore during times of crisis.
