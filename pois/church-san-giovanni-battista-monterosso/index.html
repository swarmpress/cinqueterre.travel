Description Long

The Church of San Giovanni Battista is one of the most interesting examples of Ligurian Gothic architecture in Cinque Terre. Built in the 13th century (specifically 1307), the church features a striking facade with alternating strips of white marble and green serpentine stone in the characteristic Genoese style. The bell tower was originally constructed as a defensive watch tower, part of Monterosso's medieval fortification system. The interior contains important artworks and a Baroque altar from 1744. The church stands as a testament to the village's medieval past and maritime defense history.
